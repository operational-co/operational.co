I've been building a simple SaaS service(Swipekit) since 2022. Over a period of an year, it grew to $2000 in MRR.

At the end of 2023, I sat down and went through every issue that hindered me on my way to growing Swipekit.

In the end I realised that if there was a service that would help solve my operational headaches, it would increase my productivity by a huge margin.

Every day I would come to my office, most of my time was spent dealing with these operational issues.

(insert yongfook's tweet)

I really wish something was out there that could solve all these operational headaches.

Something that would make my business operational.

Enter operational

With Operational I want to fix all these problems getting in the way of building SaaS.

I'm doing things differently this time.

For a long time I've observed api based SaaS products to offer piss-poor, or non-existent documentation. Sure they will have some docs showing you how to integrate their product, but not the why.

From my own experience, I've used Mixpanel, Amplitude, etc but I've never been able to get any meaningful insights.

I use a tool called Cleavr for server management. There's simply no documentation that shows me how to use it properly. For now, I've noted down all the steps to create a server inside Cleavr in my notes app.

With operational, documentation is going to be a very integration part of the webapp. We want you to get started.

Not only that, we want you to use the product to its fullest potential so we're going to show you how to use our product, inside your own product.

I also want to change how we communicate with our users.

For instance, I noticed that most indie hacking/startup advice on Twitter and Linkedin was utter garbage. It was all tailored towards getting more engagement anyways.

I also saw that a significant amount of companies had started using AI generated articles, leading to a drastic decrease in quality content.

Over here, I've done a couple of things to nip this in the bud.

No AI based content, ever. At most, I'll use AI to check for grammar errors.A footnote at the end of every article that explains the bias it may have.The core content won't be pandering to SEO optimization. If a article needs only 200 words, it shall be done in 200 words.Never pander to engagement on social medias.

With this in mind, operational as a brand will always be focused on:

We're going to be upfront with our customers, and tell them what they need to hear instead of whitewashing the truth.We're going to focus on getting a 1000 customers who need our help and we can help them, instead of shooting for the mass volume.

content writing 

Articles are generated, not written.

Links are sold and SEO rankings go the the highest bidder.

Posts on Linkedin and Twitter are generated to optimize for maxium engagement.

Tiktoks and other similar media are posted by creators for companies under the guide of organic content.

This is the current state of the internet.
